Hello! My name is Luis Lauro Aizpuru-Vargas and I am currently a PhD student in Computer Science (Medical Informatics Track) at Tecnológico de Monterrey. My research project focuses on how AI techniques can be leveraged to advance the personalization and automated management of sedation and analgesia in intensive care units (ICU). By studying real-time physiological signals from open datasets regarding sedation during surgeries it should be possible to draw insights into how different classification and clustering algorithms as well as their parameter fine tuning can help medical teams make more informed decisions.

I am working under the supervision of Prof. Anna Karen Gárate-Escamilla. She is on the faculty of Computer Science at the School of Engineering and Sciences . We are working in partnership with colleagues in France within the framework of Project Alison++ . The goal of this project is to create the world's first medical device that personalizes the care for critically ill patients requiring prolonged sedation. The key patners of this project are:

  • Université de Technologie de Belfort-Montbéliard (UTBM)

  • Université Marie-et-Louis-Pasteur & CHRU de Besançon

  • Cisteo Medical

Project Alison++ officially started with its kick-off meeting on September 2025. By using real ICU patient data from CHRU de Besançon the underlying mechanisms behind long term sedation can be better understood. Afterwards, software and hardware can be integrated and a personalized protocol and medical device can be manufactured. The funding for Project Allison primarily comes from the European Union structural funds via the European Regional Development Fund and is managed and co-financed by the Région Bourgogne-Franche-Comté.
